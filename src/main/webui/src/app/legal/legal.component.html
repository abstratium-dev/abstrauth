When you register an account or sign in, we collect and process the following personal data:

  • Name and e-mail address — required to create and identify your account. Legal basis: performance of a contract (Art. 6(1)(b) GDPR; Art. 31(2)(a) revDSG).

  • IP address and server logs — processed solely for network and information security (prevention of abuse, unauthorised access, and fraud detection). Legal basis: legitimate interest (Art. 6(1)(f) GDPR; Art. 31(1) revDSG). Retained for a maximum of 30 days.

We do not use your data for profiling, advertising, or any purpose other than providing and securing the authentication service.

Cookies

This service uses cookies only where strictly necessary for it to function — specifically to transport encrypted session tokens that authenticate your session. No tracking, advertising, or analytics cookies are used. The legal basis is our legitimate interest in providing a secure, stateless authentication service and, where required, your consent as provided at sign-in.

Federated Login (Google, Microsoft)

If you choose to sign in using Google or Microsoft, your browser will be redirected to that provider's authentication service. The provider will process your personal data in accordance with its own privacy policy. Abstrauth receives only the identity claims (e.g. name, e-mail, and a provider-issued identifier) returned by the provider after successful authentication. We do not share your abstrauth account data with these providers. You may use native e-mail/password authentication to avoid any data transfer to third-party providers.

Retention

Account data (name, e-mail, roles, organisation membership) is retained for as long as your account is active. Upon account deletion, personal data is erased unless we are legally required to retain it (e.g. for tax, audit, or security investigation purposes). Server logs containing IP addresses are deleted after a maximum of 30 days.
